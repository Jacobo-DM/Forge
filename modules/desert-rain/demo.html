Desert Rain

Move near the text, touch the surface, scroll, or arrive by keyboard. Words gather, brighten, and fall into reading. Adjust pace, clustering, and accessibility without changing file structure.

The desert does not speak all at once. It gathers along the edges of attention, then loosens into clusters, touch, cadence, and breath. A reader does not force the rain. The rain arrives through nearness.

Pointer movement and touch create local emergence. Scroll and keyboard arrival begin the main sequence. Reduced motion reveals words more directly and softens the rainfall effect.
