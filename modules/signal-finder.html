Purpose

A community-held emergency signal awareness system designed to help people locate surviving communication, need, and refuge signals after disaster — without turning crisis into surveillance infrastructure.

Find the signal. Protect the people.

Local Steward Map
Data belongs first to the affected community
  • Signal strength logs
  • Paper tag sightings
  • Emergency beacon locations
  • Dead-zone corridors
  • Resource points
  • Summary maps when appropriate
  • Deletion after emergency window
  • No raw export without steward consent

Data flows to a trusted local steward — a clinic, library, school, fire station, mutual-aid hub, or neighborhood council chosen by the people affected. Not assigned by outside responders or platforms.

The map belongs to the people in the map.
Codetape
Codetape is the trust layer.

Codetape means emergency signal data stays close to the people affected. The system may find signals, but authority over the map is handed to a trusted local steward — not to a cloud platform, vendor, or outside agency by default.

Codetape can include offline storage, printed handoff logs, local encryption keys, deletion protocols, paper records, and community-held access rules.

The signal can travel. The authority to hold the map does not.
  • Offline storage
  • Printed handoff logs
  • Local encryption keys
  • Deletion protocols
  • Paper records
  • Community-held access rules

Paper Layer
Design Principle
Some signs everyone knows. Some signs only this place knows.

The paper tag system carries two registers. Universal signs — a small set of emergency symbols that any person can read on arrival: water, medical help, food, safe place, danger. And local signs, created by the community before disaster season, that carry meaning specific to this street, this neighborhood, this network of people.

The paper layer is not a fallback for when wireless fails. It is a first-class protocol. It predates the internet, survives all power outages, and requires no infrastructure to receive.

Water Medical Food Safe place Danger + local symbols — designed here, before disaster season
Responsible Use Covenant
This system does not —
  • Conduct surveillance
  • Upload to cloud by default
  • Identify individuals unless rescue requires it
  • Retain data post-crisis without consent
This system does not —
  • Require account, app, login, or platform to receive help
  • Operate without a named local steward
  • Export raw data without steward consent
  • Operate outside emergency context without steward approval
